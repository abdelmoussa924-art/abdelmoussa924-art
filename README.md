## What I'm currently working on

- **Value at Risk: Normal vs Student-t Distribution**
  Comparative analysis of risk modeling approaches using Apple stock returns. I calibrate both Normal and Student-t distributions on log-returns, compute VaR95 and Expected Shortfall, and backtest both models against the 2008 crisis period. The project highlights why VaR alone can be misleading and why Expected Shortfall better captures fat-tail risk.

- **Moving Average Crossover Strategy (Microsoft)**
  Backtest of a classic MA10/MA50 crossover trading strategy on Microsoft stock, evaluated against a buy-and-hold benchmark. I track cumulative P&L, maximum drawdown, and Sharpe ratio to assess risk-adjusted performance and downside protection.

- **Stock Market Prediction Model (SPY)**
  Multiple linear regression model to predict SPY returns based on global stock market indices, exploring cross-market relationships and predictive signal construction.

## My approach

I'm interested in risk-aware quantitative analysis:

- Understanding the real shape of return distributions (fat tails, skewness) before applying risk metrics like VaR or ES.
- Backtesting strategies rigorously, looking beyond raw returns to drawdown and risk-adjusted metrics (Sharpe ratio).
- Comparing model assumptions (Normal vs Student-t) to see how they impact risk estimates in practice.
- Using statistical/ML models (regression, and exploring further techniques) to study relationships between markets.
